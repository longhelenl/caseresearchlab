Are you a speech-language pathologist with experience providing early intervention services to young children with cerebral palsy?

We’re inviting experienced SLPs to participate in a Delphi Study aiming to identify expert-informed best practices for supporting early communication development in this group of children.

What’s involved?

  • Participate in several rounds of information gathering over the next year including:
  • A brief one-to-one interview (30-45 mins)
  • Subsequent rounds of surveys (approximately 3-4, 15-20 mins each)

Who can participate?

  • Licensed or credentialed speech-language pathologists
  • At least 5 years of experience in early intervention (ages 0-3)
  • Experience working with children who are at risk for, or diagnosed with, cerebral palsy

Why participate?

  • You get the opportunity to shape recommendations and contribute your clinical expertise to an area with little existing guidance.
  • Clinicians will get access to a set of consensus-based best practices to improve communication-focused intervention for young children with complex motor needs.
  • Compensation is provided with each round of participation.
  • All participants will receive a summary of results.

Help us build a more robust, evidence-informed foundation of communication supports for children with motor disabilities in early intervention!
